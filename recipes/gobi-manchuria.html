Gobi Manchuria

Ingredients

For the cauliflower fry

  • 1 medium cauliflower (cut into florets)
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup cornflour
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for batter)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

For the Manchurian sauce

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped garlic
  • 1 tsp finely chopped ginger
  • 1-2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 small onion (cubed or chopped)
  • 1 small capsicum/bell pepper (cubed)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 1-2 tsp chili sauce
  • 1 tsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp cornflour mixed with water (slurry)
  • Spring onions (for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Wash the cauliflower florets thoroughly.
  2. Boil water with a little salt and blanch the florets for 3–4 minutes.
  3. Drain the florets and pat them dry.
  4. In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, black pepper powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  5. Add water gradually and make a smooth medium-thick batter.
  6. Add the cauliflower florets and coat them evenly with the batter.
  7. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the coated florets in batches until golden and crispy.
  8. Remove the fried florets and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  9. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok or pan.
  10. Add chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies, then sauté for a few seconds.
  11. Add onion and capsicum and stir-fry on high flame for 2–3 minutes.
  12. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, chili sauce, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  13. Mix well and cook for 1 minute.
  14. Add the cornflour slurry and stir continuously until the sauce becomes glossy and slightly thick.
  15. Add the fried cauliflower and toss well until evenly coated with the sauce.
  16. Garnish with spring onions.
  17. Serve hot as a dry starter or with gravy alongside fried rice or noodles.
